I am currently a lab manager at the Visual Perception and Cognition lab at the Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. I work on adaptation in eye movements. I have also recently started working on computanional neuroanatomy, we are trying to map the intra parietal sulcus using diffusion MRI. Before coming here I was a Research Assistant at the Center for Cognitive Science, Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, India, where I also completed my Bachelor's in Biological Science & Bio-engineering. Some of the questions I like to think about are- What are the nature of the representations which allow for visual perception? How are these representations categorised? How do these representations interact with new incoming stimuli? In short, how do we see?

Most of my earlier projects make use of visual search tasks. Prof. Amitabha Mukerjee introduced me to visual search during a course on Cognitive Science. Following which, I had the privelege to work with Prof. Jeremy Wolfe at Harvard Medical School on modelling Hybrid Foraging (a variant of visual search). Then in the summer of 2016 I got the opportunity to work with Prof. Jérôme Sackur at École Normale Supérieure, Paris, modelling the subjective response of participants performing a visual search task. Since August 2016, I have enjoyed working with Dr. Devpriya Kumar, on the influence of top-down processes on visual search performance (our work has been accepted to OPAM 2017 Yay!).

When not working on the unraveling the mysteries of the human mind, I can be found reading a book, training for a triathlon or watching Arsenal FC loosing yet another match.

I love all of science in general and love to talk and learn about new stuff thanks to lovely friends who are spread over different fields. Some topics that I hope to explore soon are information theory, compressive sensing, mathematical logic, theory of computation (lamda calculus), complex systems (genetic algorithms), quantum mechanics, fluid dynamics (let's solve the Navier Stokes) and more. Its probably going to take a lifetime, but that's what we are here for, right? I have been strongly influenced by the works of: Bertrand Russell and Francis Crick.
